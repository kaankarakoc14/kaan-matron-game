How to Play

  1. Players take turns. The game starts with Player 1.
  2. On your turn, select a number from the board.
  3. The number you select is added to your score.
  4. All unselected factors of that number are given to the other player and added to their score.
  5. Both the selected number and its factors are removed from the board.
  6. If you select a number that has no remaining factors on the board:
    • You score no points
    • You lose your turn
    • The number stays on the board
  7. The game ends when no number on the board can form a factor relationship with any other number, or when there are no numbers left.
  8. The player with the highest score wins.

Example: If Player 1 selects 12, they get 12 points. Player 2 then automatically gets the proper factors of 12 (1, 2, 3, 4, and 6) and receives 16 points (1+2+3+4+6).

Game Modes

2 Players: Two human players take turns playing the game.

vs Computer: Play against the AI with three difficulty levels:

  • Easy: The computer makes random valid moves.
  • Medium: The computer chooses better moves based on a points ratio.
  • Hard: The computer uses advanced strategies and considers future moves.
